Dynamics of Land Use

Previous Next

Study of land cover and use in Tocantins, between the years 1990 and 2007, developed between Geoambiente, Seplan, under World Bank financing. This study has generated an extensive and unprecedented base of information on the spatial dynamics of land use in Tocantins, especially in relation to anthropized landscapes - that is, which have suffered the intervention of man, due to agricultural activities.

From the digital classification using PCI Geomatica, interpretation of Landsat-5 satellite images through ArcGIS and field surveys to recognize the landscapes, hundreds of maps were generated and crossed with other data - economic and social.

It was observed that in 1990, 74% of the State's territory was covered by Cerrado vegetation, while in 2007, this index dropped to 63%. This means that 11% of the vegetation cover that existed in Tocantins was altered by the action of Man.
