Viability of Wildlife Corridor

Previous Next

This study aimed to analyze the connectivity between environments in the directly affected area of ​​a mining and its surroundings, in the sense of a RPPN (Private Natural Heritage Reserve) located in the north of Minas Gerais State, Brazil, verifying continuous forest remnants, which may favor the natural dispersion of fauna and flora.

Eight factors were decisive in determining this viability: hydrography; buildings; urban distance; loss of natural vegetation over time; gain of natural vegetation over time; land use and coverage; fragment size (largest patch index) generated on Fragstats, distance to highways. The hierarchization of these was done using the AHP method, while processing and visualization through ArcGIS software.
